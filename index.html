    • Psychological Well-being
      Spending prolonged periods in the confined and isolated environment of a spacecraft can take a toll on mental health. Establishing routines, staying connected with loved ones through video calls, and participating in recreational activities can help alleviate feelings of loneliness and monotony. Additionally, utilizing psychological support resources provided by mission control or engaging in mindfulness techniques can aid in managing stress and promoting overall psychological well-being.

    • Adherence to Safety Protocols
      In space, even minor mishaps can have severe consequences due to the harsh and unforgiving environment. Strict adherence to safety protocols and procedures is paramount to ensure the well-being of the crew and the success of the mission. Prioritize safety drills, familiarize yourself with emergency procedures, and consistently follow guidelines for handling equipment, conducting experiments, and performing spacewalks. Vigilance and attention to detail are essential to mitigate risks and maintain a safe working environment onboard the spacecraft.
